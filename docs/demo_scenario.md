# Demo Scenario

## Accounts to use

- `admin` — full administrative access;
- `security_officer` — training owner and report reviewer;
- `employee` — regular learner;
- optional CSV import users from `docs/examples/org_structure_import.csv`.

## Demonstration order

1. Open the homepage and show the project scope.
2. Log in as `security_officer`.
3. Show the course catalog and assigned learning path.
4. Show assignments and the quiz flow.
5. Open the dashboard and reports.
6. Open the audit journal.
7. Open the integrations page and explain CSV import.
8. Switch to `employee` and show the personal view.
9. Switch to `admin` and show administrative access.

## What to show the teacher

- that the system supports mandatory IB training, not a generic LMS;
- that training is tied to roles, departments, and positions;
- that reports are available for control purposes;
- that audit logging exists for sensitive actions;
- that data import is prepared for corporate org structure sync.

## How to explain the difference from a typical LMS

- this is a compliance-focused platform;
- assignments are driven by role and organizational structure;
- completion is tracked together with quiz results and deadlines;
- reporting and auditing are first-class features.

## Reports to show

- dashboard;
- employee report;
- department report;
- course report;
- CSV export.

## How to show audit and personal data protection

- open the audit journal and point to assignment / quiz / export events;
- explain that only privileged roles can view it;
- show that profiles use department and position references, not free text;
- mention role-based access control for reports and management pages.

## How to show org-structure integration

- open the integrations page;
- show the CSV format;
- show the demo import file;
- explain that LDAP / Active Directory are prepared as placeholders for future sync.
