# Economic Effect

## What the project saves

The system reduces manual work for three groups:

- **Security officer**: no need to track assignments, deadlines, quiz outcomes, and exports in spreadsheets.
- **HR specialists**: no need to manually coordinate the corporate structure and learner lists for each training cycle.
- **Department managers**: no need to request status updates one by one; they can review reporting data centrally.

## Example calculation

Assume the organization has:

- 120 employees;
- 1 mandatory training cycle per quarter;
- 1 security officer;
- 3 department managers involved in control;
- 1 HR specialist maintaining employee lists.

### Before implementation

Approximate manual effort per cycle:

- security officer: 8 hours on assignment tracking, reminders, and summary reports;
- HR specialist: 4 hours on updating employee lists and coordinating structure changes;
- department managers: 3 hours total on collecting status information.

Total per cycle: **15 hours**.

If the average blended hourly cost is 1,200 RUB, the cost is:

**15 × 1,200 = 18,000 RUB per cycle**

For 4 cycles per year:

**72,000 RUB per year**

### After implementation

With automated assignments, quizzes, reports, audit logging, and CSV import:

- security officer: 2 hours per cycle;
- HR specialist: 1 hour per cycle;
- department managers: 0.5 hour per cycle.

Total per cycle: **3.5 hours**

Annual cost:

**3.5 × 1,200 × 4 = 16,800 RUB per year**

### Estimated savings

**72,000 − 16,800 = 55,200 RUB per year**

This is a conservative example. In larger organizations the effect grows because:

- the number of employees increases;
- the number of training programs grows;
- manual control becomes more expensive.

## Qualitative effect

- transparency of control improves;
- overdue training risk decreases;
- reporting becomes unified and repeatable;
- auditability increases;
- the organization can prove that mandatory IB training was assigned, taken, and tracked.
